Three fires before us.
Two burn with a
stirring sputter 
in the other room,
laughing and blowing ash
on our furniture.
The other fire lives 
starry and happily elsewhere.
 
They make us dry
or hot with anger
or sunburnt and sorry.
But love is not a flame.
It is kindling
And we have plenty.
