1. Question: 
What is a Belief?

1a. Why is this question worth asking:
We all believe things. We believe many things. In this sense, it is a shared experience. Becuase beliefs are things we all experience, to understand what they are, what they are for, how they can be gained, changed, lost, dropped and used, is to understand some facet of us (humans, people, believing beings).

1b. What do I expect to do with the conclusion?
I hope to gain some insight into my own beliefs, where they come from, what forces change them or maintain them. I hope, throguh that lense to better understand my friends and family and collegues and neighbors. To have minute glimpe into how they think, or make decisions, or why they do what they do. 

More broadly I am probably hoping to to gain some insight into "how the world works" or rather why it's working like _this_, so seemingly badly. We are at each others throats. I suspect it's because we all think we know what others believe and have judged their beliefs incorrect, or dangerous, or bad. But I don't think we know what we believe much of the time. We only _believe_ that we know.

If any of this is true, maybe sharing this insight will help someone, somewhere _see_ their fellow and judge them more slowly, because they have a certain empathy about the difficulty or mystery of belief.

2. Possible Answers

2a. What are my kneejerk answers or ideas toward the Question?
What do I think about belief?

* We all believe things, or we all have beliefs.
* We believe many things.
* We take many beliefs for granted.
* We don't always know what we believe, or we don't know all of our beliefs all of the time.
* We don't know what others believe.
* Our beliefs change, morph, fall away, appear suddenly, emerge slowly.
* We act on what we believe.
* Our beliefs affect our bodies, our emotions, our relationships, our morals and values, and the Earth.

* A belief can be true, at least true enough, for the believer at the time of belief.
* A belief can be false.
* A belief is not obvious.
* A belief can be good or bad.
* A belief can be doubted.
* A belief can be challenged.
* A belief may be, inherently, challenged. (otherwise it's just a fact?)
* A belief is held.
* A belief can be easy to hold, or difficult to hold.
* A belief can be in harmony or conflict with other beliefs.
* A belief can be in conflict with other beliefs.
* A belief can be nurtured, by yourself, by others.
* A belief can be a core part of one's identity.
* A belief is a ball. It can be tossed around, held up, up in the air, dropped, picked up, offered to another, lobbed at another, thrown at another.
* A belief can be shared.
* A belief can be hidden, even or especially to oneself.
* A belief can be practical. Perhaps _only_ or always practical.
* It is a tool.
* It is useful to the believer.
* It can limit the believer.
* It limits reality.
* It frames reality.
* It clarifies reality.
* It reveals what's possible.
* It instructs in the ways of the world.
* It can be exploited.
* Biases are built out of belief. 

* It is important to know _that_ we believe.
* It is less important to know _what_ we believe.
* It is more important to know _why_ we believe.
* It is important to know that others believe. 
* It is important to understand others don't necessarilly know what they believe. 
* One might gain insight if they can catch a belief in the act.
* It would be good to _gently_ help others do the same.
